SSP205

Pathway to STEM PhD I

Course Overview

SSP205: Pathway to STEM PhD I is designed to provide students with the foundational knowledge and skills necessary for pursuing advanced degrees in STEM fields. This course explores critical issues in STEM research ethics, intersectionality, and the importance of diverse perspectives in scientific advancement.

Students will engage with historical case studies, contemporary research, and interactive discussions to develop a deeper understanding of the complex relationship between science, society, and identity.

Course Modules & Resources

Module 1: Foundations of Intersectionality in STEM

This module examines historical examples of ethical issues in scientific research and introduces the concept of intersectionality as a framework for understanding the complex interactions between identity and scientific practice.

  • The Story of Henrietta Lacks: Medical Ethics and Systemic Racism (LabXchange)
  • The Tuskegee Syphilis Study
  • Syphilis Study Committee Report
  • Mappings the Margins - Kimberlé Crenshaw
  • A Brief Review of the Belmont Report
  • Henrietta Lacks and America's Dark History of Research Involving African Americans

Module 2: Global Perspectives and Decolonizing STEM

This module explores how STEM knowledge is produced across different cultural contexts and examines efforts to decolonize scientific research and education. Students will consider how diverse knowledge systems can contribute to scientific innovation and ethical practice.

Readings and resources will be added as the course progresses.

Module 3: Empowering Diverse Voices in STEM

This module examines strategies for creating more inclusive STEM environments and amplifying underrepresented voices in scientific research and discourse. Students will analyze case studies of successful inclusion initiatives and develop their own proposals for fostering diversity in STEM fields.

Readings and resources will be added as the course progresses.

Module 4: Reimagining Research through Diverse Perspectives

This module challenges students to consider how diverse perspectives can lead to innovative research questions, methodologies, and applications. Students will explore examples of how inclusive approaches have led to breakthroughs in various STEM fields and will develop frameworks for incorporating diverse perspectives into their own research.

Readings and resources will be added as the course progresses.
